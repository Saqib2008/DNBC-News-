US Dollar continues to strengthen against Pakistani rupee | DNBC News

By DNBC News | 21 May 2024

The US dollar's continued strengthening against the Pakistani rupee reflects ongoing economic challenges and global market dynamics impacting Pakistan's currency. This trend has significant implications for various sectors of the Pakistani economy and the daily lives of its citizens.â€‹

Several economic factors contribute to the weakening of the Pakistani rupee against the US dollar. Persistent trade deficits, high inflation rates, and substantial external debt obligations have put pressure on the rupee. Additionally, political instability and economic policy uncertainties exacerbate these challenges, leading to decreased investor confidence and capital outflows.

The depreciation of the rupee makes imports more expensive, leading to higher costs for imported goods such as fuel, machinery, and raw materials. This can increase production costs for local industries, contributing to inflation and affecting the affordability of goods for consumers. On the flip side, a weaker rupee can make Pakistani exports more competitive in international markets, potentially boosting export revenues. However, the benefits are often offset by increased input costs due to expensive imports.

A stronger dollar contributes to inflationary pressures in Pakistan as the cost of imported goods rises. This affects the prices of essential items such as food and fuel, which can have a disproportionate impact on lower-income households. The central bank may face challenges in balancing efforts to control inflation with measures to stimulate economic growth.

Pakistan's significant foreign debt, much of which is denominated in US dollars, becomes more expensive to service as the rupee depreciates. This increases the burden on the national budget and can limit the governmentâ€™s ability to invest in essential public services and development projects.

On a positive note, the depreciation of the rupee can increase the value of remittances sent by Pakistanis working abroad. These remittances, which are a crucial source of foreign exchange for the country, can provide some relief by boosting household incomes and supporting domestic consumption.

To address the weakening rupee, the government and the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) might implement various policy measures. These can include tightening monetary policy to control inflation, seeking financial assistance from international institutions like the IMF, and promoting policies to enhance export competitiveness and attract foreign investment.
