A 5-day national anti-polio campaign has started in Jhelum from today | DNBC News

By DNBC News | 24 April 2024

In an ongoing effort to combat the spread of polio, a critical national anti-polio campaign has commenced in Jhelum, Pakistan, today. The five-day initiative aims to immunize children against the crippling virus, furthering the nation's commitment to eradicating polio once and for all.

Polio, a highly infectious viral disease, primarily affects young children, leading to irreversible paralysis in severe cases. Despite significant progress in reducing polio cases globally, Pakistan remains one of the few countries where the virus persists, posing a persistent threat to public health.

The launch of the anti-polio campaign in Jhelum underscores the government's unwavering determination to reach every child with the polio vaccine, particularly in high-risk areas. Health workers, volunteers, and government officials are mobilizing resources and efforts to ensure the success of the campaign, which involves door-to-door vaccination drives and outreach activities.

Amidst challenges such as vaccine hesitancy, misinformation, and security concerns, the campaign in Jhelum emphasizes community engagement and awareness-raising initiatives to build trust and confidence in the polio vaccine. Health authorities are collaborating closely with religious leaders, community elders, and influencers to address misconceptions and encourage vaccination acceptance.

The success of the anti-polio campaign hinges on the collective efforts of government agencies, healthcare providers, community leaders, and civil society organizations. Strict monitoring and surveillance mechanisms are in place to track vaccination coverage and identify areas requiring additional intervention.

The anti-polio campaign in Jhelum is part of a broader national immunization strategy aimed at reaching every child with lifesaving vaccines. Beyond polio, Pakistan continues to prioritize routine immunization against diseases such as measles, diphtheria, and tuberculosis, safeguarding the health and well-being of future generations.

As the anti-polio campaign unfolds in Jhelum over the next five days, the collective efforts of stakeholders and the dedication of frontline workers will be instrumental in achieving the campaign's objectives. By vaccinating children against polio, Pakistan moves closer to the ultimate goal of a polio-free future, ensuring that every child has the opportunity to lead a healthy and fulfilling life.
