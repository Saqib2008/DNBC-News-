Early voting for May 28 primary runoff elections will go on | DNBC News

By DNBC News | 20 May 2024

Despite concerns surrounding the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, early voting for the May 28 primary runoff elections in Texas is set to proceed as scheduled. This decision comes after the Texas Supreme Court rejected a request to expand mail-in voting to all registered voters amid the pandemic, reaffirming the existing regulations.â€‹

In Harris County, which encompasses Houston, there has been a notable surge in the number of requested mail-in ballots for the primary runoffs, indicating a heightened level of interest and engagement among voters. This heightened interest underscores the importance of these elections, particularly in determining nominees for various significant positions, including the U.S. Senate, the U.S. House, and the Texas Railroad Commission.

To ensure the safety of voters and poll workers, Harris County has implemented rigorous safety measures at polling locations. These measures include the provision of hand sanitizer, the encouragement of social distancing, and frequent sanitization of voting equipment and surfaces. Such precautions are crucial in mitigating the risk of COVID-19 transmission during the voting process.

Early voting locations are set to open on May 18, providing voters with ample opportunity to cast their ballots ahead of the official Election Day on May 28. This extended early voting period aims to accommodate diverse schedules and reduce congestion at polling places on Election Day itself.

Given the critical decisions to be made in these primary runoffs, it is imperative that Texans exercise their right to vote. Early voting offers a convenient and safer alternative for those who wish to avoid potential crowds and long lines on Election Day. By participating in early voting, Texans can play an active role in shaping the future of their communities and the state as a whole.

Moreover, the record-breaking number of requested mail-in ballots indicates a growing recognition of the significance of these elections, both locally and nationally. As such, voters are encouraged to familiarize themselves with the candidates and issues at stake, ensuring that their votes reflect their values and priorities.

In these unprecedented times, the resilience of the democratic process is evident as communities adapt and innovate to uphold the integrity of elections while prioritizing public health and safety. As early voting commences, Texans have the opportunity to make their voices heard and participate in shaping the future trajectory of their state and country.
