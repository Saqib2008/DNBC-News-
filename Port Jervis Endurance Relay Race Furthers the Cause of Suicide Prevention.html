Port Jervis Endurance Relay Race Furthers the Cause of Suicide Prevention | DNBC News

By DNBC News | 14 May 2024

The Port Jervis Endurance Relay Race, known as the Midnight Madness Endurance Relay, is an impactful event dedicated to raising awareness and funds for suicide prevention. Scheduled for April 20-21, 2024, the race is set on the beautiful watershed trails of Port Jervis, New York. Participants will run a 3-mile loop continuously from 6 PM to 6 AM, either solo or in relay teams of up to four people. The event promises a night filled with running, camaraderie, and activities such as music, movies, and food at the base camp, creating a community-driven atmosphere aimed at supporting mental health initiatives​​

The event honors the memory of Jim Faggione Jr., a local Port Jervis athlete and former school district food service director, who battled mental health issues and tragically ended his life in 2022. Jim was an avid runner who envisioned an endurance race on the Port Jervis trails. The Midnight Madness Relay not only fulfills his dream but also serves as a poignant reminder of the importance of addressing mental health issues. Proceeds from the race are split between the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention and the Outdoor Club of Port Jervis, ensuring that the funds benefit both mental health initiatives and the maintenance of local trails​​.

Participants and supporters alike are motivated by the cause, which aims to shed light on the silent struggles many face with mental health. The event underscores the importance of community support in addressing these challenges. Suicide prevention is a critical issue, and events like the Midnight Madness Relay play a vital role in fostering awareness and providing necessary resources for those in need​.

The Midnight Madness Endurance Relay offers a unique experience for runners and supporters. While runners tackle the trail loops, they also have opportunities to rest, enjoy music and movies, and share stories by the campfire, creating a supportive environment that emphasizes teamwork and resilience. This setup not only makes the event enjoyable but also strengthens the communal bond among participants, reinforcing the collective effort to combat mental health issues and prevent suicide​.

Overall, the Midnight Madness Endurance Relay is more than just a race; it is a community effort to honor a beloved local figure, raise vital funds for important causes, and bring people together in the fight against mental health issues and suicide. The event's dual focus on physical endurance and mental health awareness makes it a powerful platform for change, embodying the spirit of perseverance and solidarity​.

The Midnight Madness Endurance Relay is part of a broader effort to address the pervasive issue of mental health struggles within the community. By highlighting the story of Jim Faggione Jr., the race aims to break the stigma surrounding mental health and encourage open conversations about these critical issues. Participants and supporters are reminded of the importance of mental well-being and the impact that collective community actions can have on preventing tragedies like suicide. This race serves not only as a memorial for Jim but also as a catalyst for change, inspiring others to seek help and support each other​.

Additionally, the funds raised by the Midnight Madness Endurance Relay contribute significantly to both the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention and the Outdoor Club of Port Jervis. These organizations benefit from the financial support to continue their essential work: the former in providing mental health resources, education, and support for those affected by suicide, and the latter in maintaining and enhancing the local trails that bring the community together. By supporting these causes, the event creates a lasting positive impact on both mental health awareness and the preservation of local natural resources, embodying a holistic approach to community well-being​ .
