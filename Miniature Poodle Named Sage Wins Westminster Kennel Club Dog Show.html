A New York appeals court has upheld a gag order against former President Donald Trump, which was initially imposed by Judge Arthur Engoron during Trump’s civil fraud trial. This gag order restricts Trump and his legal team from making public comments about the judge’s staff. The appellate court ruled that Trump’s legal team used the incorrect procedural method to challenge the gag order. Instead of filing an Article 78 proceeding, which is designed to challenge specific judicial actions, they should have pursued a traditional appeal process​

Trump’s attorneys argued that the normal appellate process would be too slow to address the ongoing harm to Trump’s First Amendment rights. However, the appeals court noted that the harm posed by the gag order was minimal due to its narrow scope​​​.

The gag order was reinstated on November 30 after a temporary stay, following reports of threats and harassment directed at Judge Engoron and his staff, which had decreased when the gag order was in effect​.

The civil fraud trial, initiated by New York Attorney General Letitia James, accuses Trump and his organization of inflating the value of assets to secure favorable loans and tax benefits. The trial concluded its testimony phase recently, with closing arguments scheduled for mid-January 2024​ ​.

Trump’s legal challenges extend beyond this specific gag order, as he faces multiple trials and legal battles. The civil fraud case, brought by New York Attorney General Letitia James, is just one element of a broader array of legal issues confronting the former president. These include charges related to attempts to overturn the 2020 election results, for which he is under a separate gag order in a federal case in Washington, D.C. This D.C. gag order aims to prevent Trump from making disparaging comments about key witnesses, prosecutors, or court staff that could influence the trial’s proceeding​.

Trump’s legal team has consistently argued that these gag orders infringe on his First Amendment rights, framing them as judicial overreach intended to silence his free speech. Despite these arguments, the courts have maintained that the gag orders are necessary to protect the integrity of the judicial process and ensure the safety of court personnel. The New York appeals court's decision underscores the judiciary's stance on balancing free speech rights with the need to prevent potential harm and maintain order during high-profile legal proceedings​.

This gag order is one of several legal constraints Trump faces amid various legal battles, including a separate gag order in a federal case related to the 2020 election interference​ .
