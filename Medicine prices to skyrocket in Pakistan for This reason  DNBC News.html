Medicine prices to skyrocket in Pakistan for This reason | DNBC News

By DNBC News | 19 May 2024

The prices of medicines in Pakistan are expected to skyrocket due to several interrelated economic pressures. The country is grappling with significant inflationary trends, driven by a combination of factors including the depreciation of the Pakistani rupee, rising import costs, and increased production expenses. These economic challenges are severely impacting the pharmaceutical sector, leading to higher costs for essential medicines.â€‹

The sharp depreciation of the Pakistani rupee against major currencies, particularly the US dollar, is a primary driver of increasing medicine prices. Since a substantial portion of pharmaceutical raw materials and finished products are imported, the cost of these imports has risen dramatically in local currency terms. This has forced pharmaceutical companies to pass on the increased costs to consumers, resulting in higher prices for medicines.

In addition to currency depreciation, rising production costs are contributing to the spike in medicine prices. The costs of raw materials, energy, and transportation have all surged, partly due to global supply chain disruptions and higher fuel prices. Pharmaceutical manufacturers are facing higher operational expenses, which are subsequently being reflected in the retail prices of medicines.

Regulatory challenges also play a role in the escalating prices. Delays in the approval process for new drugs and pricing adjustments by the Drug Regulatory Authority of Pakistan (DRAP) have created uncertainty and financial strain for pharmaceutical companies. The regulatory environment needs to become more responsive to the economic realities faced by the industry to help stabilize prices.

The anticipated rise in medicine prices poses a serious threat to healthcare accessibility in Pakistan. For many low- and middle-income households, higher medicine costs could lead to reduced access to essential medications, potentially worsening health outcomes. This situation underscores the need for urgent policy interventions to mitigate the impact on vulnerable populations.

The government is under pressure to address the issue of soaring medicine prices. Potential measures include providing subsidies or financial assistance to pharmaceutical companies, improving the efficiency of regulatory processes, and exploring ways to stabilize the currency. Additionally, encouraging local production of pharmaceuticals through incentives and reducing dependency on imports could be long-term solutions to control prices.

The expected increase in medicine prices in Pakistan is driven by a complex interplay of economic pressures, including currency depreciation, rising production costs, and regulatory challenges. This situation poses significant risks to public health, particularly for economically disadvantaged populations. Addressing these challenges requires coordinated efforts from the government, regulatory bodies, and the pharmaceutical industry to ensure that essential medicines remain accessible and affordable for all citizens.
