EU investigates Meta over Facebook and Instagram child safety | DNBC News

By DNBC News | 12 May 2024

The European Union has launched an investigation into Meta over concerns regarding child safety on Facebook and Instagram. The probe focuses on whether Meta has done enough to protect children on these platforms, amid accusations that it has historically failed to implement sufficient safeguards. Critics argue that Meta prioritized growth over safety, leaving minors vulnerable to harmful content and exploitation.​

This investigation is part of a broader effort by the EU to enforce stricter regulations under the Digital Services Act (DSA), which mandates enhanced protections for users, particularly minors. The DSA applies to major platforms with over 45 million monthly users, including Instagram and Facebook, and aims to hold these companies accountable for user safety.

Meta has stated that it is committed to creating safe online environments for teens and has implemented various measures, such as restricting direct messaging between adults and minors and using advanced technologies to detect and remove harmful content. However, the EU's inquiry will scrutinize whether these steps are sufficient and effectively enforced​.

The EU's investigation into Meta over child safety on Facebook and Instagram highlights several key concerns. The primary issue is whether Meta's current safety measures sufficiently protect minors from exposure to harmful content, such as bullying, sexual exploitation, and inappropriate material. Despite Meta's claims of using advanced technology and policies designed to safeguard young users, the effectiveness and enforcement of these measures are under scrutiny​.

Meta has faced criticism for its algorithms, which have been accused of promoting content that can negatively impact the mental health of young users. Reports have suggested that Instagram's algorithms, in particular, can push content related to body image issues, leading to anxiety and depression among teenagers. This has prompted calls for more stringent controls and transparency in how content is managed and recommended to minors.

The EU's investigation is part of a broader initiative to ensure that large tech companies comply with the Digital Services Act (DSA), which aims to create a safer digital environment. The DSA mandates that platforms must take proactive measures to protect users, especially minors, from illegal and harmful content. The act also requires these platforms to be more transparent about their content moderation practices and to provide detailed reports on their efforts to remove harmful content​.

As the investigation progresses, Meta will likely face increased pressure to demonstrate that it can effectively protect young users. This could lead to more stringent regulations and possibly substantial fines if the company is found to be non-compliant with the DSA. The outcome of this investigation will not only impact Meta but also set a precedent for other social media platforms operating within the EU, potentially leading to broader reforms in how these companies address child safety online​.​.
